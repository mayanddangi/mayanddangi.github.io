Hey, I am Mayand !

As a young technology enthusiast, I have a deep passion for the development of quantum hardware and am passionate about exploring the cutting-edge fields of quantum computing, photonics, semiconductors, and embedded systems. I am particularly fascinated by the potential of quantum technology to revolutionise the way we process and analyse information. In pursuit of this passion, I have gained valuable research experience working in the Cold Atom Trapping Laboratory under the guidance of Prof. Bodhaditya Santra at IIT Delhi. During this time, I have had the opportunity to contribute to the design and characterization of optical tweezers for the cold atom trapping of cesium atoms. In addition, I have been actively involved in the development of external cavity diode lasers, which has helped me to strengthen my practical skills in laser technology. My previous research experience includes working with Prof. Megha Bhatt at the Physical Research Laboratory in Ahmedabad, where I had the opportunity to analyse and characterise the lunar polar regions using Chandrayaan-2 DFSAR data. I am particularly proud of my contribution to this project, which involved the development of an algorithm for orthorectification of SAR images. This was a great achievement, as only ISRO had the data pipeline for this task. As someone who is deeply committed to using technology to better society, I am excited about the potential of quantum technology to solve some of the most pressing problems facing our world today. Projects and Experience

Optical Tweezers for trapping atoms

Designing of Optical Tweezers• July'22 - Present

We focused on the development of optical tweezers for the trapping of cold atoms in quantum computing. The spatial light modulator was utilised to generate optical tweezers, and the Gerchberg-Saxton method was employed to calculate the best phase pattern for a certain trap setup. We recently created a program to reconfigure our trap configuration in order to make the trapped array defect-free using the Hungarian algorithm.

External Cavity Diode Lasers

Designing of External Cavity Diode Laser• September'21 - Present

Designed and constructed the custom-designed external cavity diode laser, as well as developed its complex electronics systems.

DFSAR Chandrayaan-2

Analysis and Characterization of Lunar Polar Regions using Chandrayaan-2 DFSAR data • May '22 - July '22

In this project, our objective was to use orthorectification to eliminate geometrical distortions from the Chndrayaan-2 DFSAR polar image. Out of a variety of orthorectification techniques, we have chosen the range doppler approach to carry out orthorectification using backward geocoding. Our developed methodology produced results with a structural similarity index that ranges from 74% to 96% relative to the original data provided by ISRO.

XLR-20 Race Car

Embedded System Design Engineer • September '19 - September '21

Designed and fabricated custom designed Electric Control Units based on STM32F0 and STM32F4 microcontroller for intra-vehicle communication and for managing and manipulating the received data from various installed sensors of the vehicle. Implemented three-nodes CAN (Controller Area Network) communication Protocol between STM32F4 based ECUs. Developed software for data retrieval from sensors & its manipulation, data communication between ECUs and for controlling inverters on STM32F4 Microcontroller. Introduced new isolation technology to reduce the bulkiness and increase performance in design for isolating PWM signals by using Digital Isolator.
